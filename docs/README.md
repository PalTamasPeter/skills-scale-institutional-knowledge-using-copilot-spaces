# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

OctoAcme follows a structured, customer-first project lifecycle emphasizing iterative delivery, clear ownership, and data-informed decisions. The process spans five key phases: **Initiation** (validating business need), **Planning** (breaking work into shippable increments), **Execution** (building and testing with daily standups), **Release** (standardized deployment), and **Close & Retrospective** (capturing learnings).

Three primary personas drive delivery: **Project Managers** coordinate schedules and communications, **Product Managers** define value and prioritize work, and **Developers** implement features with quality. Each role has clearly defined responsibilities to ensure smooth collaboration and accountability across the entire project lifecycle.

Quality is enforced through unit/integration tests, smoke tests, and CI/CD pipelines, ensuring that every release meets OctoAcme's standards before reaching customers. Execution relies on daily standups, sprint-based tracking, and clear definition-of-done criteria to keep teams aligned and delivery predictable.

Risk management is proactive with weekly reviews of the Risk Register, transparent status reporting, and structured retrospectives enabling continuous improvement. By combining rigorous process with open communication, OctoAcme teams can respond quickly to change while maintaining high standards of delivery.
